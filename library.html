Featured book of the month


The Midnight Library" by Matt Haig: In "The Midnight Library," the protagonist, Nora Seed, finds herself in a mystical library between life and death, where each book represents a different version of her life based on the choices she could have made. As Nora explores these alternate lives, she gains profound insights into her regrets, dreams, and the value of her existence. It's a moving tale about the power of choice and the search for meaning in life.

Background of the author

Matt Haig, the author of The Midnight Library, is a British novelist and journalist born on July 3, 1975, in Sheffield, England. He is well-known for his ability to blend elements of speculative fiction with deep psychological insights, often exploring themes of mental health, depression, and the human condition.Haig's early life was marked by a significant struggle with depression, which he has openly discussed in his writings and interviews. At the age of 24, he suffered a severe depressive episode that nearly led him to take his own life. This experience profoundly shaped his perspective and later became a central theme in his work.

Before The Midnight Library, Haig authored several successful books, including Reasons to Stay Alive, a memoir that offers a candid exploration of his battle with mental illness and has been widely praised for its honest and hopeful message. He has also written other novels like The Humans and How to Stop Time, both of which explore existential themes and the complexities of life.The Midnight Library, published in 2020, became a bestseller and resonated with readers around the world for its exploration of regret, possibility, and the many different lives one might live. The novel's central concept—that between life and death, there is a library where one can explore different versions of one's life—captures Haig's interest in the "what ifs" of life and the impact of our choices

In addition to his novels and non-fiction, Haig has also written children's books, often imbuing them with similar themes of empathy, understanding, and the importance of mental well-being. Haig continues to be a prominent voice in discussions around mental health, often using his platform to advocate for greater awareness and support. His writing is known for its accessibility, warmth, and depth, making complex emotional and philosophical ideas relatable to a broad audience.
